During my journey, I was confronted with problem situations (technical problem solving, strategic choices ...) during which I had to implement a relevant practice to achieve a desired result. Communication

Presentation

The Communication module is really vast, as it covers some aspects from MAC layers and protocols, to emerging networks and technologies with 5G. It also deals with the issue of energy for connected objects and embedded systems. The main problem for me in this training unit was the workload and the fact that it is not my field of expertise which implies that I needed more time to assimilate the concepts. The fact that this unit of formation is divided into five sub-parts makes it it rich and interesting, but also makes its difficulty.

Resolution

To overcome this problem, I had to work hard and particularly I had to read up on the topics discussed. The mandatory research works helped me in understanding some important points. Hopefully, I already have some expertise in some of the covered topics, as Energy for Embedded Systems, because I already took this cours in 4th year. It also allowed me to concentrate on the subjects I mastered the least.

Knowledge & skills

This training helped me improve my networking skills as well as my knowledge about communication technologies (3G, 4G, 5G, Zigbee, LoRa, Sigfox,...). I also reviewed my competences on signal modulation and telecommunications through the Software-defined radio lab. The Emerging Networks course allowed me to acquire knowledge and develop initial skills in configuring SDN equipment and developing network control applications on an SDN infrastructure. Even if the course took place at the very end of the semester, I could also learn the basic notions of security for IoT networks.

Review

This training unit was very complete, and I learned a wide range of skills. This training unit was also very various in terms of learning methods: it was composed of lectures, practical work and labs, research work and discussions. This is the training unit in which I have made the most progress due to the plurality of the courses.

Middleware and services

Presentation

This training unit is software-oriented and was thus very new to me. But the course format with online videos and labs allowed me to keep pace. At first, I encountered some difficulties with the settings of Virtual Machines and dockers. I also struggled at first on the OM2M part because I did not master the basics yet.

Resolution

Thanks to peer exchange and the research work I made, I managed to set up correctly the VM network. For the OM2M part, I managed to set up the sensor networks with some help and personnal work.

Knowledge & skills

This module, through the resolution of the many software problems I encountered, allowed me to learn a lot about middlewares in general. I could deploy and configure and IoT architecture using OM2M, and interact with the different resources of the architecture using REST services. I learned how to address HTTP requests, and how to build a dashboard with NodeRed. It was a good way for me to refresh my memory of the Java coding. As I did not know much about IoT before, I also learned how to situate the main standards for the Internet of Things.

Review

Concerning learning methods, this training units was mainly based on MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses). I liked this format, because it allowed me to work at my own rythm. I appreciated it more than lectures, because you can just pause the videos if you need more time to take notes, and you can also replay. I also liked much the lab format of the Cloud and autonomic management course, which allowed us to start with a strong theorical part and move on rapidly to practise. Finally, this training unit was a lot about IoT itslef and I consider it as central to the training.
