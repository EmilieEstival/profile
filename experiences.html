Internship

Environment and context

Thales Alenia Space designs and delivers satellites for telecommunications, the navigation, Earth observation and management environmental, exploration, science and technology, and orbital structures. This internship was carried out at LPE department, in the Radiation Test team. In space, the cosmic rays and the solar flares are carriers of heavy ions, heavy metals and protons and neutrons. When these particles collide with a microelectronic device (processor) of the satellite, that may cause a strong current surge due to a short circuit (called latchup) and therefore potentially cause the destruction of the component.

Assigned missions

My mission was to develop a PCB for a test bench in order to monitor latchup events in components such as FPGA under radiations. Several parameters had to be taken into acccount: assembly, size, integration and easy to use. My achievements were the following:

  • Realization of the system under CAD software electronic (length: 25 pages)
  • Creation of a library of symbols to represent project-specific components PCB routing, ready to be launched in production after review (width: 10 layers)
  • Creation of a bookshop of component cavities for link the representation of the diagram to the physical representation on the PCB.

Innovative Project

Environment and context

This project was carried out during the 5 months of the last INSA year. The goal of this project is to design an architecture for a flight simulator with VR headset and dynamic seat. The idea is to build a simulator to show the know-how of INSA students (demonstration at the JPO, lab support, etc.). An internship has already been carried out to create the software part of the flight simulator, and to link this representation with the virtual reality headset. A proof of concept (POC) was made from this realization, which is functional.

Assigned missions

My missions concerned 2 major parts in this project: the controller part, and the communication one. More precisely, I had to:

  • Choose the controllers' architecture

  • Specify the communication protocol and header

Reasearch work on communication and protocols for connected objects

Environment and context

This research work was carried out within the framework of the UF 'Communication'. The first study was carried out about Bluetooth technology, which is a wireless networking protocol designed to quickly and remotely connect devices to computers or to each other. Bluetooth devices automatically detect and connect to each other, making communication much easier. Bluetooth Low Energy technology is a very low-power alternative for sensors and accessories. It is ideal for applications that do not require a continuous connection, but require a long life battery.

The wireless sensor networks (WSNs) are used in a wide range of applications to capture, gather and analyze data from their environment. The wireless sensor network architecture typically consists of a large number of small battery-powered sensor nodes displayed over an area of interest and forming a multi-hop communication network. In a wireless sensor network sensors nodes are a low cost, resource constrained devices. Due to their location and possibly difficulty of access, energy efficiency is an important requirement in a medium access control (MAC) protocol for most wireless sensor networks. This was the object of the second study.

Assigned missions

The mission was to succeed in documenting the requested protocols from reliable sources and to synthesize the result in the form of a report of a few pages. The reports for both parties can be found just below.
