Internship

Environment and context

Thales Alenia Space designs and delivers satellites for telecommunications, the navigation, Earth observation and management environmental, exploration, science and technology, and orbital structures. This internship was carried out at LPE department, in the Radiation Test team. In space, the cosmic rays and the solar flares are carriers of heavy ions, heavy metals and protons and neutrons. When these particles collide with a microelectronic device (processor) of the satellite, that may cause a strong current surge due to a short circuit (called latchup) and therefore potentially cause the destruction of the component.

Assigned missions

My mission was to develop a PCB for a test bench in order to monitor latchup events in components such as FPGA under radiations. Several parameters had to be taken into acccount: assembly, size, integration and easy to use. My achievements were the following:

  • Realization of the system under CAD software electronic (length: 25 pages)
  • Creation of a library of symbols to represent project-specific components PCB routing, ready to be launched in production after review (width: 10 layers)
  • Creation of a bookshop of component cavities for link the representation of the diagram to the physical representation on the PCB.

Clean room training to produce a nanoparticule gaz sensor (Smart Devices)

Environment and context

Chemical synthesis of WO3 nanoparticles and integration on microelectronic devices for the realisation of a gas sensor. Evaluation of sensor performance under controlledatmosphere.

Assigned missions

The objective is to produce a gas sensor based on WO3 nanoparticles and then to evaluate it under the following conditions controlled gaseous atmosphere to provide the datasheet. The process then breaks down into 4 milestones :

  • Synthesis of WO3 nanoparticles
  • Development of the microchip
  • Integration of the sensitive nanoparticle layer on the chip
  • Electrical characterisation of the sensor under controlled atmosphere
The final gas sensor is to be integrated to become a "smart sensor". To do so, I had to design a PCB in KiCad in order to have on the same board the produced sensor, a commercial sensor as well as an Arduino Uno. A circuit analysis was conducted on LTSpice before the integration.

Reasearch work on communication and protocols for connected objects

Environment and context

This research work was carried out within the framework of the UF 'Communication'. The first study was carried out about Bluetooth technology, which is a wireless networking protocol designed to quickly and remotely connect devices to computers or to each other. Bluetooth devices automatically detect and connect to each other, making communication much easier. Bluetooth Low Energy technology is a very low-power alternative for sensors and accessories. It is ideal for applications that do not require a continuous connection, but require a long life battery.

The wireless sensor networks (WSNs) are used in a wide range of applications to capture, gather and analyze data from their environment. The wireless sensor network architecture typically consists of a large number of small battery-powered sensor nodes displayed over an area of interest and forming a multi-hop communication network. In a wireless sensor network sensors nodes are a low cost, resource constrained devices. Due to their location and possibly difficulty of access, energy efficiency is an important requirement in a medium access control (MAC) protocol for most wireless sensor networks. This was the object of the second study.

Assigned missions

The mission was to succeed in documenting the requested protocols from reliable sources and to synthesize the result in the form of a report of a few pages. The reports for both parties can be found just below.

Introductory research project

Environment and context

Over the last decade, artificial intelligence has become an active field of research. Even though its use is growing exponentially, only few truly understand what is at stake. The definition of artificial intelligence as well as its challenges and the issues it raises are still very vague notions to be understood by most. As the effectiveness of non-scientific tools to spread scientific knowledge has been proved, our challenge is to popularize artificial intelligence and refute all prejudices around it in a playful way. The focus is mainly on students of middle and high school. Keeping that in mind, we designed an escape game on the theme of AI. An escape game is a game to be played in a group built around a succession of puzzles, most of the time to solve a situation or to escape a place. Since the idea of this project is to make the game accessible to all, the setup uses as few materials as possible, including objects you can easily have access to (smartphone, paper, blackboard, etc.). We decided to make a virtual version of the game, so that everyone can be able to play online. It is focused on a development suitable for schools and universities. All the puzzles composing the escape game revolve around a background story, with a problem to solve.

Assigned missions

To better understand what is at stake with Artificial Intelligence, we conducted a 6 months research work about AI history, techniques and ethical questions it raises. The report provides an overview of Artificial Intelligence and discusses definition and history, the main techniques of AI algorithms, as well as its current use in society. Possibilities and estimations on AI future developments are shared. Then, the project of developing an online escape game based on AI is presented.
